ENGLISH GRAMMAR RULES & PATTERNS (A1-C2)

=== VERB TENSES ===

PRESENT SIMPLE (A1)
- Use: Habits, facts, routines
- Form: Base verb / He/She/It + -s
- Examples:
  ✓ I play football every day
  ✓ She works in London
  ✓ Dogs have four legs
- Negatives: do/does + not + verb
  ✓ I don't like coffee
  ✓ He doesn't play chess

PRESENT CONTINUOUS (A1)
- Use: Actions happening now
- Form: am/is/are + verb-ing
- Examples:
  ✓ I am eating breakfast
  ✓ She is reading a book
  ✓ They are playing tennis
- Negatives: am/is/are + not + verb-ing
  ✓ I'm not watching TV

PAST SIMPLE (A1)
- Use: Completed actions in the past
- Form: verb + -ed (regular) OR irregular forms
- Examples:
  ✓ I watched a film yesterday
  ✓ She went to Paris last week
  ✓ They played football in the park
- Common irregular verbs: go/went, see/saw, eat/ate, have/had
- Negatives: did + not + base verb
  ✓ I didn't see him yesterday

PAST CONTINUOUS (A2)
- Use: Actions that were happening at a specific time in the past
- Form: was/were + verb-ing
- Examples:
  ✓ I was sleeping when you called
  ✓ They were dancing at the party
- Often used with past simple: While he was reading, she arrived

PRESENT PERFECT (A2)
- Use: Actions with results in present, life experiences, recent events
- Form: have/has + past participle
- Examples:
  ✓ I have lived here for 5 years
  ✓ She has never traveled to Asia
  ✓ We have just finished breakfast
- Common question: Have you ever...?

FUTURE SIMPLE (A1)
- Use: Decisions, predictions, facts about the future
- Form: will + base verb
- Examples:
  ✓ I will help you tomorrow
  ✓ She will definitely pass the exam
  ✓ It will be sunny next week
- Negatives: will not (won't)

BE GOING TO (A2)
- Use: Plans, intentions, predictions based on evidence
- Form: am/is/are + going to + base verb
- Examples:
  ✓ I'm going to study English tonight
  ✓ Look! It's going to rain
  ✓ We're going to visit Paris in summer

PAST PERFECT (B1)
- Use: An action that happened before another past action
- Form: had + past participle
- Examples:
  ✓ When I arrived, they had already left
  ✓ By the time she graduated, she had lived in 3 countries
- Sequence: Past Perfect → Past Simple

=== CONDITIONAL SENTENCES ===

ZERO CONDITIONAL (A2)
- Form: If + present simple, present simple
- Use: General truths, natural laws
- Examples:
  ✓ If you heat water to 100°C, it boils
  ✓ If it rains, the ground gets wet

FIRST CONDITIONAL (A2)
- Form: If + present simple, will + base verb
- Use: Possible situations in the future
- Examples:
  ✓ If it rains, I will stay at home
  ✓ If you work hard, you will succeed

SECOND CONDITIONAL (B1)
- Form: If + past simple, would + base verb
- Use: Imaginary situations (unlikely or impossible now)
- Examples:
  ✓ If I won the lottery, I would travel the world
  ✓ If you studied harder, you would pass the exam

THIRD CONDITIONAL (B1)
- Form: If + past perfect, would have + past participle
- Use: Imaginary situations in the past (impossible to change)
- Examples:
  ✓ If I had known about the party, I would have gone
  ✓ If she had studied, she would have passed the test

=== ARTICLES ===

DEFINITE ARTICLE (THE) (A1)
- Use: 
  ✓ Specific items: The book on the table is mine
  ✓ Unique things: The sun, the moon, the president
  ✓ Second mention: I saw a dog. The dog was brown
  ✓ Musical instruments: play the piano, play the guitar
- Countries with "the": The USA, The UK, The Netherlands

INDEFINITE ARTICLES (A/AN) (A1)
- A + consonant sounds: a dog, a university (sounds like "yoo-")
- An + vowel sounds: an apple, an hour (silent h)
- Use: First mention of countable nouns
  ✓ I saw a cat yesterday

ZERO ARTICLE (A2)
- Plural countable nouns (general): Dogs are loyal animals
- Uncountable nouns (general): Water is essential for life
- Proper nouns: Paris is a beautiful city (not "the Paris")
- Meals: Have breakfast / Eat lunch

=== REPORTED SPEECH ===

STATEMENTS (B1)
- Direct: She said, "I am tired"
- Reported: She said (that) she was tired
- Tense shift: present → past, past → past perfect
- Example patterns:
  ✓ He said "I work here" → He said he worked there
  ✓ She said "I have finished" → She said she had finished

QUESTIONS (B1)
- Direct: She asked, "Where do you live?"
- Reported: She asked where I lived
- Word order: Changes to statement form
- Example: He asked "Can you help?" → He asked if I could help

IMPERATIVES (B1)
- Direct: "Close the door!" she said
- Reported: She told him to close the door / She asked him to close the door
- Use tell/ask + object + infinitive

=== PASSIVE VOICE ===

SIMPLE PASSIVE (A2)
- Form: be + past participle
- Use: Focus on the action, not the doer
- Examples:
  ✓ Active: Someone designed this house
  ✓ Passive: This house was designed in 1990
  ✓ Passive: Football is played worldwide

FOCUS: By whom? (by-agent optional)
- The book was written by Shakespeare
- The car was repaired by a mechanic

=== SUBJECT-VERB AGREEMENT ===

SINGULAR SUBJECTS (A1)
- Takes -s form: He works, She plays, The dog barks
- Singular nouns: One person, A team is...
- Uncountable nouns: Water is essential

PLURAL SUBJECTS (A1)
- Takes base form: They work, Dogs bark
- Plural nouns: People are friendly

COLLECTIVE NOUNS (B1)
- British English: The team ARE playing (plural verb)
- American English: The team IS playing (singular verb)
- Examples: group, team, family, class, government

=== WORD ORDER ===

BASIC SENTENCE ORDER (A1)
Subject + Verb + Object + Adverbial information
- I like coffee in the morning
- She plays tennis every weekend
- They arrived at the station yesterday

ADJECTIVE PLACEMENT (A1)
Adjectives before nouns: a beautiful day, the interesting book
Multiple adjectives: Opinion + Size + Age + Color + Origin + Material
- a beautiful old Spanish house
- a small round wooden table

ADVERB PLACEMENT (A2)
- Adverbs of frequency: usually, often, sometimes, rarely
  ✓ I always drink tea (before main verb)
  ✓ I am always late (after be verb)
- Adverbs of manner: at end of clause
  ✓ She speaks English fluently
- Adverbs of time: at beginning or end
  ✓ Yesterday, I saw her / I saw her yesterday

QUESTIONS (A1)
Yes/No: Do you like ice cream?
Wh- questions: What, Where, When, Who, Why, How
- What do you do? / Where does she live? / When did they arrive?
- Inversion: Auxiliary + Subject + Main verb
  ✓ Do you work here? (not "You do work here?")

=== NEGATION ===

WITH AUXILIARY VERBS (A1)
- Do/Does/Did + not + verb
  ✓ I don't like coffee
  ✓ He doesn't work here
  ✓ They didn't go yesterday

WITH BE (A1)
- am/is/are/was/were + not
  ✓ I'm not tired
  ✓ She wasn't at home

WITH MODALS (A2)
- Modal + not
  ✓ I can't swim / I cannot swim
  ✓ She won't help
  ✓ They shouldn't arrive late

DOUBLE NEGATION (B1)
- Avoid in standard English: NOT "I don't see nothing"
- Correct: I see nothing / I don't see anything

=== COMMON ERRORS ===

❌ "I am boring" → ✓ "I am bored" (passive adj)
❌ "Interesting in" → ✓ "Interested in" (after be)
❌ "He don't work" → ✓ "He doesn't work" (3rd person -s)
❌ "I am agree" → ✓ "I agree" (no be with this verb)
❌ "I have 25 years" → ✓ "I am 25 years old" (age expression)
❌ "How long you live here?" → ✓ "How long have you lived here?" (present perfect for duration)
