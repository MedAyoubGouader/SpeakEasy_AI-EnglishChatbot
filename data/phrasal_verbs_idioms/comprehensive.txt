ENGLISH PHRASAL VERBS & IDIOMS (A2-C2)

=== PHRASAL VERBS WITH GET ===

GET UP (Wake and leave bed)
- Example: I get up at 7 AM every morning
- Noun form: I need to do a get-up now
- Cannot separate: âœ“ Get up! (NOT get up the bed)

GET ON (Board vehicle / Have good relationship)
- Board: Get on the bus / train / plane
- Relationship: They get on well together
- Question: How do you get on with your colleagues?
- Phrasal noun: a good get-on with someone

GET OVER (Recover from problem/illness)
- Example: It took months to get over her illness
- Example: She couldn't get over the shock
- Similar: Get over it! (accept and move on)

GET AWAY (Escape / Take vacation)
- Escape: The thief got away from police
- Vacation: We need to get away for a weekend
- Expression: Get away with (not be punished for)
- Example: He got away with cheating on the test

GET DOWN (Become sad / Write down)
- Sad: The bad news got me down
- Write: Get down what I'm saying
- Physical: Get down on the floor

GET OFF (Leave vehicle / Escape punishment)
- Vehicle: Get off the bus at Oxford Street
- Escape: He got off without punishment
- Physical contact: Get your hands off me!

GET BACK (Return / Recover / Revenge)
- Return: Get back home by 10 PM
- Recover: I'll get my money back
- Revenge: Get back at someone (take revenge)
- Together: Get back together (reconcile)

GET IN/GET OUT (Enter/Exit)
- In: Get in the car / Get in the house
- Out: Get out of bed / Get out of trouble
- Expression: Get out! (leave immediately)

GET THROUGH (Complete / Communicate)
- Complete: Get through the work
- Contact: Can't get through to him (phone)
- Endure: Get through difficult times
- With object: Get through to someone

GET ALONG (Progress / Have good relationship)
- Progress: How are you getting along?
- Relationship: We get along well

GET ROUND (Avoid / Come to topic / Move around)
- Avoid: There's no getting round it (cannot avoid)
- Come to: Get round to doing (eventually do)
- Move: Get round the obstacle

=== PHRASAL VERBS WITH LOOK ===

LOOK AT (Direct attention toward)
- Example: Look at this beautiful view!
- Example: Look at the time - we're late!
- Cannot move: NOT look the picture at

LOOK FOR (Search)
- Example: I'm looking for my keys
- Example: What are you looking for?
- Cannot move: NOT look keys for

LOOK AFTER (Care for)
- Example: Who will look after the children?
- Example: Can you look after my bag?
- Separable: Look after them / Look them after (less common)

LOOK UP (Search in book / Improve)
- Book: Look up the word in dictionary
- Improve: Business is looking up
- Separable: Look up the number / Look the number up

LOOK DOWN ON (Despise)
- Example: He looks down on poor people
- Attitude: She looks down on anyone less educated
- Meaning: Regard as inferior

LOOK FORWARD TO (Anticipate with pleasure)
- Example: I'm looking forward to the weekend
- Example: Looking forward to seeing you!
- Requires -ing: NOT look forward to see

LOOK OUT (Be careful / Watch)
- Warning: Look out! Car coming!
- Watch: Look out for any mistakes
- Separable: Look out for danger / Look danger out for (rare)

LOOK LIKE (Resemble / Appear)
- Appearance: She looks like her mother
- Appearance of situation: It looks like rain
- These socks look like they're new

=== PHRASAL VERBS WITH PUT ===

PUT ON (Wear / Switch on device / Gain weight)
- Wear: Put on your coat / Put your coat on
- Device: Put on the light / Put on the TV

- Separable: Put on the shoes / Put the shoes on

PUT OFF (Postpone / Discourage)
- Postpone: Put off the meeting until Friday
- Discourage: Don't let him put you off
- Separable: Put off the exam / Put the exam off

PUT UP (Construct / Tolerate / Provide accommodation)
- Build: Put up a tent / Put up a building
- Tolerate: I won't put up with that behavior
- Accommodation: Can you put me up for a night?
- Separable: Put up the painting / Put the painting up

PUT AWAY (Store / Imprison)
- Store: Put away your toys
- Imprison: They put him away for years
- Separable: Put away the dishes / Put the dishes away

PUT DOWN (Write / Criticize / Kill animal)
- Write: Put down your thoughts
- Criticize: Stop putting me down!

- Separable: Put down your pen / Put your pen down

PUT OUT (Extinguish / Produce / Inconvenience)

- Produce: The company put out a new product

- Separable: Put out the fire / Put the fire out

PUT UP WITH (Tolerate)
- Example: I can't put up with this noise
- Example: How do you put up with him?

=== PHRASAL VERBS WITH TAKE ===

TAKE AFTER (Resemble in appearance/personality)
- Example: She takes after her mother
- Example: He takes after his father in personality
- Cannot separate: NOT take mother after

TAKE AWAY (Remove / Subtract)
- Remove: Take away the plates
- Subtract: 5 take away 3 equals 2
- Separable: Take away the food / Take the food away

TAKE BACK (Return / Retract statement)
- Return: Take back the damaged goods
- Retract: I take back what I said
- Separable: Take back your words / Take your words back

TAKE DOWN (Write / Disassemble)
- Write: Take down the details
- Remove structure: Take down the tent
- Criticize: Don't try to take me down
- Separable: Take down the number / Take the number down

TAKE IN (Understand / Give accommodation / Consume)
- Understand: Did you take in what he said?
- Accommodation: Take in refugees
- Consume: Take in calories
- Separable: Take in the washing / Take the washing in

TAKE OFF (Remove / Aircraft departs / Become popular)
- Remove: Take off your shoes
- Plane: The plane takes off at 9 AM
- Popular: The business took off
- Separable: Take off your hat / Take your hat off

TAKE ON (Accept responsibility / Hire / Face opponent)
- Responsibility: Take on a project
- Hire: We're taking on new staff
- Opponent: Take on the champion
- Separable: Take on the job / Take the job on

TAKE OVER (Assume control)
- Example: New manager takes over next week
- Example: The company was taken over by a rival
- Cannot separate: NOT take over responsibility the company

TAKE UP (Occupy space/time / Start hobby)
- Space: The sofa takes up too much room
- Time: That takes up 2 hours
- Hobby: Take up tennis
- Cannot separate
