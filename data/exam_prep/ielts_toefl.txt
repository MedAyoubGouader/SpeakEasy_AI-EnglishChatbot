IELTS & TOEFL EXAM PREPARATION (B1-C2)

=== IELTS OVERVIEW ===

IELTS Structure
- Academic vs General Training (2 versions)
- 4 sections: Listening (30 min), Reading (60 min), Writing (60 min), Speaking (11-14 min)
- Scoring: 0-9 bands (9 = native proficiency)
- Total test duration: ~3 hours
- Results valid: 2 years

BAND SCORES EXPLAINED
- 9.0 (Expert) - Near native-like proficiency
- 8.0 (Very Good) - Occasional hesitation/errors
- 7.0 (Good) - Generally accurate, occasional problems
- 6.0 (Competent) - Generally effective communication despite some inaccuracies
- 5.0 (Modest) - Can manage simple communications
- Below 5.0 - Limited English ability

BAND REQUIREMENTS BY INSTITUTION
- University admission: 6.5-7.5 typically
- Professional licensing: 6.0-7.0
- Job positions: 5.5-6.5

=== LISTENING SECTION ===

Format: 4 recordings (10 questions each = 40 total)

SECTION 1: Conversational (everyday situation)
- Restaurant booking / Hotel inquiry / Doctor appointment
- Accents: Various English accents
- Speed: Natural, conversational pace
- Task types: Multiple choice, matching, form completion

SECTION 2: Monologue (educational/training)
- Museum tour / Lecture excerpt / Workplace presentation
- Task types: Multiple choice, note completion, labeling

SECTION 3: Academic discussion (3-4 speakers)
- Student-tutor discussion / Group project meeting
- Academic topics
- Task types: Multiple choice, note completion

SECTION 4: Academic lecture
- Specialized topics
- Task types: Multiple choice, summary completion, note-taking

LISTENING STRATEGIES
1. Preview questions (know what to listen for)
2. Predict answers (guess format: names, numbers, places)
3. Use contextual clues (tone, surrounding information)
4. Don't obsess over one question (continue, come back later)
5. Check spelling/grammar in answers
6. Practice with authentic materials (BBC, etc.)

COMMON ANSWER FORMATS
- Names, places, numbers (specific details)
- Dates, times (temporal information)
- Opinions, reasons (why someone does something)
- Descriptions (adjectives for people/places/things)

=== READING SECTION ===

Academic Reading (1300-1500 words across 3 passages)

PASSAGE 1: General interest topic
- ~400 words
- Task type: Multiple choice or matching headings

PASSAGE 2: More specialized content
- ~500 words
- Task types: T/F/NG (True/False/Not Given), matching

PASSAGE 3: Most advanced/technical
- ~800 words
- Task types: Multiple choice, short answer, summary completion

COMMON TASK TYPES

Multiple Choice
- 4 options (A, B, C, D)
- Paraphrase/rephrasing of text
- Careful reading required

True/False/Not Given
- True: Agrees with text
- False: Contradicts text
- Not Given: Information not in text (NOT "I don't know")

Matching Headings
- Find appropriate heading for each paragraph
- One heading is extra
- Read paragraphs carefully

Summary/Note Completion
- Fill blanks with words from text
- Limited word count (usually max 2-3 words)
- Must be from text (not paraphrase)

READING STRATEGIES
1. Skim passage titles and headings first (overview)
2. Scan for specific information (key words in questions)
3. Paraphrase while reading (understand, not memorize)
4. Don't translate (read in English)
5. Time management: 20 min per passage
6. Check answers fit context

DIFFICULT READING PATTERNS
- Implicit information (requires inference)
- Word paraphrase (same meaning, different words)
- Opinion vs fact (author's view vs stated facts)

=== WRITING SECTION ===

TASK 1: Graph/Chart/Process Description (20 min)
- 150 words minimum
- Describe data, trends, or processes
- Formal register
- Clear structure with paraphrasing of task

TASK 2: Essay (40 min)
- 250 words minimum
- Argument, opinion, problem-solution, or discussion essay
- Formal academic style
- Clear thesis and supporting arguments

ESSAY TYPES

Opinion Essay
- Prompt: "Do you agree or disagree?"
- Structure: Introduction + thesis, 2-3 body paragraphs, conclusion
- Show both views briefly, take clear position

Discussion Essay
- Prompt: "Discuss both views"
- Structure: Introduction, view 1, view 2, conclusion
- No need to state personal opinion (or do subtly)

Problem-Solution Essay
- Prompt: "What are the problems? What solutions?"
- Structure: Introduction (problem), body (problems), body (solutions), conclusion
- Real or hypothetical problems

Advantage-Disadvantage Essay
- Prompt: "Discuss advantages and disadvantages"
- Structure: Introduction, advantages paragraph, disadvantages paragraph, conclusion

WRITING ASSESSMENT CRITERIA
1. Task Achievement (40%) - Addresses all parts fully
2. Coherence & Cohesion (25%) - Logical flow, linking words
3. Lexical Resource (25%) - Vocabulary accuracy, range
4. Grammatical Accuracy (25%) - Correct structures, variety

COMMON COHESIVE DEVICES
- Addition: furthermore, moreover, in addition
- Contrast: however, on the other hand, nevertheless
- Cause-effect: because, as a result, therefore
- Example: for example, for instance, such as
- Conclusion: in conclusion, to summarize, finally

=== SPEAKING SECTION (11-14 minutes) ===

PART 1: Introduction (4-5 minutes)
- Topics: Home, family, hobbies, work, studies
- 8-10 questions
- Show natural speech, don't memorize

PART 2: Individual presentation (3-4 minutes)
- Describe person/place/object/experience
- 1 minute preparation with prompt card
- Talk for 2 minutes
- Answer follow-up question

PART 3: Discussion (4-5 minutes)
- Abstract questions related to Part 2
- Opinion questions
- Higher-level vocabulary/structures

SPEAKING ASSESSMENT
- Fluency & Coherence (25%) - Speaks smoothly, uses linking words
- Lexical Resource (25%) - Vocabulary range and accuracy
- Grammatical Accuracy (25%) - Complex structures, variety
- Pronunciation (25%) - Clear, understandable, stress/intonation

COMMON MISTAKES
- Memorized answers (sounds unnatural)
- Speaking too fast (comprehension problems)
- Repeating question exactly (rephrase it)
- Simple vocabulary (try more advanced words)
- Not using contractions naturally (I'm, it's, won't)

SPEAKING STRATEGIES
1. Use fillers naturally (well, you know, I mean)
2. Give full answers (extend beyond yes/no)
3. Explain your answers (give examples, reasons)
4. Self-correct naturally (that is, in other words)
5. Maintain eye contact with examiner
6. Speak at natural pace (not too fast/slow)

=== TOEFL OVERVIEW ===

TOEFL Structure (Internet-based)
- 4 sections: Reading (54-72 min), Listening (41-57 min), Speaking (17 min), Writing (50 min)
- Scoring: 0-120 (30 per section)
- Computer-based only (as of recent changes)
- Duration: ~3 hours
- Results valid: 2 years

TOEFL vs IELTS
- TOEFL: US-focused, academic focus, computer-based
- IELTS: UK-focused, general/academic, pen-paper or computer

=== TOEFL READING ===

Format: 3-4 passages (250-300 words each)
- Time: 54-72 minutes
- 10 questions per passage
- Total: 30-40 questions

QUESTION TYPES

Multiple Choice (single answer)
- 4 options
- Reading comprehension

Multiple Choice (multiple answers)
- Check all that apply
- 3-4 correct answers

Matching (reference)
- Match pronouns to nouns they reference

Filling blanks
- Complete sentences with proper word forms

Inserting sentence
- Add missing sentence to correct location

=== TOEFL LISTENING ===

Format: 2 types of audio
- Conversations (2 speakers, 2-3 minutes)
- Lectures (1 speaker, 4-6 minutes)

Questions: 5 per conversation, 6 per lecture
- Multiple choice (1 answer)
- Multiple choice (multiple answers)
- Notes/summary completion

LISTENING CHALLENGES
- American accent primarily
- Pronunciation variations
- Background noise/authentic speech
- Complex academic content
- Can't replay audio (unlike IELTS)

=== TOEFL SPEAKING ===

4 Tasks (17 minutes total)

TASK 1: Opinion (15 sec prep, 45 sec speak)
- Personal opinion: Do you prefer X or Y?
- Reasons and examples

TASK 2: Integrated (30 sec prep, 60 sec speak)
- Read passage, listen to lecture, respond

TASK 3: Integrated (30 sec prep, 60 sec speak)
- Read passage, listen to conversation, respond

TASK 4: Integrated (20 sec prep, 60 sec speak)
- Listen to lecture, respond

SCORING: 0-30
- Delivery (pronunciation, pace, fluency)
- Language use (grammar, vocabulary)
- Topic development (completeness, coherence)

=== TOEFL WRITING ===

2 Tasks (50 minutes total)

INTEGRATED TASK (20 minutes)
- Read passage, listen to lecture (contradicting)
- Write 150-225 words explaining relationship
- Summarize, don't give opinion

INDEPENDENT TASK (30 minutes)
- Essay on given topic
- 300+ words recommended
- Opinion or experience

COMMON TOEFL WRITING TOPICS
- Education (importance of certain subjects, learning methods)
- Technology (impact on communication, society)
- Money/Work (importance of money, career choices)
- Society (traditions, cultural change)
- Personal (qualities, experiences, values)

=== EXAM TIPS FOR BOTH ===

TIME MANAGEMENT
- IELTS Reading: 20 min per passage = 60 min total
- IELTS Writing: 20 min Task 1 + 40 min Task 2
- TOEFL: Follow time allocations per section

VOCABULARY BUILDING
- Learn collocations (groups of words that go together)
- Study academic word list (AWL)
- Practice synonyms/paraphrasing
- Read academic articles, listen to lectures

STRESS MANAGEMENT
- Arrive 15 minutes early
- Deep breathing before test
- Read questions carefully (don't rush)
- Answer what you can, move on if stuck
- Review if time allows

PRACTICE MATERIALS
- Official practice tests (highest priority)
- BBC Learning English
- TED talks with transcripts
- Academic journals and articles
- Podcasts and audiobooks

MINIMUM REQUIRED SCORES
University undergraduate: 6.0 (IELTS) / 61 (TOEFL)
Graduate programs: 6.5-7.0 (IELTS) / 80-95 (TOEFL)
Professional licensing: Varies (often 7.0+ IELTS)
